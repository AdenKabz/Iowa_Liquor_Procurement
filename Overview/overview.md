# Outlook of Iowa Liquor Procurement

## Introduction

(image)

The liquor industry of Iowa is tightly controlled by the state through Iowa Alcoholic Beverages Division (IABD). Iowa is one of 18 liqour control states in the US [^1]. Before any distilled alcoholic beverage gets handled by the end customer, it undergoes strict screening and approval from the state. All licensed store retailers are then able to update their inventory levels by purchasing from the state (the sole wholesaler). According to Find Recovery (2025), justice is surely served when it comes to alcohol consumption in Iowa as the state has the greatest proportion of adults engaging in excessive drinking with a binge drinking rate of 22.5% [^2]. Binge drinking based on CDC's surveys is considered consuming 5 or more drinks in one ocassion for men or 4 or more drinks for women [^3]. This certainly does not mean that binge drinking is an everyday thing but it gives perspective as to why store retailers could be enticed to up their game in procurement.
