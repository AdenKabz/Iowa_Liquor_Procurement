# Outlook of Iowa Liquor Procurement

## Introduction

![Iowa Liquor Supply Chain](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/IOWA%20LIQUOR%20SUPPLY%20CHAIN.jpg)

The liquor industry of Iowa is tightly controlled by the state through **Iowa Alcoholic Beverages Division (IABD)**. Iowa is one of 18 liqour control states in the US [^1]. Before any distilled alcoholic beverage gets handled by the end customer, it undergoes strict screening and approval from the state. All licensed store retailers are then able to update their inventory levels by purchasing from the state (the sole wholesaler). According to Find Recovery (2025), justice is surely served when it comes to alcohol consumption in Iowa as the state has the greatest proportion of adults engaging in excessive drinking with a binge drinking rate of **22.5%** [^2]. **Binge drinking** based on CDC's surveys is considered consuming 5 or more drinks in one ocassion for men or 4 or more drinks for women [^2]. This certainly does not mean that binge drinking is an everyday thing but it gives perspective as to why store retailers could be enticed to up their game in procurement. This outlook is meant to uncover what procurement looked like overall from 2016 to 2025. <br><br>


*Core Question*: What is the overall outlook of Iowa liquor procurement? <br><br>

## Procurement Overview

![Overview](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/overview.png)
[Tableau Link - Overview](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/Overview?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- In the **10-year period (2016-2025)**, the **overall daily recorded transactions** were **24,661,604**, **procurement expenditure** amounted to **over $3.8 billion** and the number of individual **bottles procured totaled 283,348,898**.
- A **case** is usually the container or box the distributor or wholesaler uses to package the individual bottles, which is then transported to the retail store. Based on the data, **a total of 26,472,719 cases were procured**.
- The standard measure of alcoholic beverages is milliliters (ml) but in this a uniform base (liter - a liter being 1000ml) was adopted. The **overall volume liters** that was procured according to the data was **227,227,431**. In addition, the **overall average price per liter** was noted to be **$17.03** which means that most liquor bottles fell under the **Standard tier** (More on this later).

## Order Size Category
This is used to group together the different ranges of bottles as procured by retail stores. The table below paints the picture for the categorization dimensions involved:

![Order Size Category Metrics](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/order%20size%20category.png)
- **Boutique fill (between 6 and 24 bottles per day)** dominates in overall procurement expenditure, bottles procured and transactions.
- **Massive allocation (more than 600 bottles per day)** is the minority in the category with the lowest numbers of procurement expenditure, bottles procured and transactions.

## Product Premium Tier
This is used to classify alcoholic beverages secured based on their price per liter. The table below helps to put everything into perspective:

![Product Premium Tier Metrics](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/pdt%20premium%20tier.png)
- **The Standard tier (whose price per liter falls between $15 and $30)** has attracted the most procurement expenditure while the **Economy tier (whose price per liter is way below $15)** has garnered the greatest number of transactions and bottles procured.
- **The Ultra-Premium tier (whose pice exceeds $50)** ranks the lowest overall in terms of total procurement expenditure, bottles procured and transactions.

## Pack
This can simply be thought of as a bundle of liquor bottles. A pack often contains the same brand of equal bottle sizes bundled together. Retail stores usually have a choice to decide whether a case should come containing individual bottles or one or more packs. 

![A Jack Daniel 6-pack](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/6-pack%20Jack%20Daniel.jpg)

The following table displays the **top 10 packs** that have dominated the procurement scenes in Iowa (2016-2025):

![Top 10 Packs](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/pack%20metrics.png)
- The **12 pack (12 bottles)** and **6 pack (6 bottles)** are the **top 2** in the Iowa market overall in terms of procurement expenditure, bottles procured and transactions with the **12 pack** being the highest.
