# Time Analysis

## Introduction
The discourse makes an effort to uncover insights from the **yearly**, **monthly**, **seasonal** and **weekly** trends of procurement in Iowa spanning the **10 year period (2016-2025)**. <br><br>

<ins>Core Question</ins>: *How does Iowa liquor procurement trend across each time dimensions, that is, year, month, season and day of the week?* <br><br>

## Yearly Trends

![Year - Key Metrics](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/year%20metrics.png)
[Tableau Link - Yearly Analysis](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/Year-KeyMetrics?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- In terms of **total transactions**, a steady rise is witnessed from **2016** to **2021** before dropping slightly and reaching its peak in **2023**.
- For **total procurement expenditure**, an gradual increase is seen from **2016** all the way to the peak year, **2024** before dropping in **2025**.
- When it comes to **total bottles procured**, a steady increase is seen from **2016** to **2021**, drops but regains momentum and reaches its peak in **2024**.
- In relation to **total cases procured**, a gradual increase is witnessed from **2016** to **2021** before dropping a bit and reaching its peak in **2023**.
- For **total volume procured liters**, a steady rise is seen from **2016** all the way to the peak year, **2021** before experiencing a dip till 2025.
- **Post 2024** generally saw a decline across **all procurement metrics**.
- The **overall average price per liter** rose from **2016** to **2024 (peak year)** before dropping in **2025**.
- **Total transactions** and **total cases procured** follow the same pattern across the years. Conversely, **total bottles procured** and **average price per liter** also follow the same pattern across the years.

## Monthly Trends

![Month - Key Metrics](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/month%20metrics.png)
[Taleau Link - Monthly Analysis](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/Month-KeyMetrics?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- Across **all procurement metrics**, **December** is the peak month.
- For **total procurement expenditure**, **total bottles procured** and **total cases procured**, there is consistent rise from **January** to **June**.
- For **total transactions**, the rise is witnessed from **February** to **June** while in the case of **total volume procured liters**, the rise stretches from **January** to **May**.
- **January** consistently has the **lowest procurement figures** in terms of total procurement expenditure, bottles procured, cases procured and volume procured liters. **February** instead has the **lowest number of transactions** overall. In addition, **May** has the **lowest average price per liter**.

## Seasonal Trends

![Season - Key Metrics](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/season%20metrics.png)
[Tableau Link - Seasonal Analysis](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/Season-KeyMetrics?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- **Winter** has the **least procurement figures** in terms of total transactions, procurement expenditure, bottles procured, cases procured and volume procured liters where as **Spring** has the **least average price per liter**.
- **Summer** captures the **highest numbers** of **total transactions**, **total bottles procured** and **total volume procured liters**.
- **Autumn** indicates the **highest numbers** of **total procurement expenditure**, **total cases procured** and **average price per liter**.

## Weekly Trends

![Day of Week - Key Metrics](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/weekday%20metrics.png)
[Tableau Link - Weekly Analysis](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/DayOfWeek-KeyMetrics?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- The **weekend (Sunday and Saturday)** has **very low procurement** being undertaken in terms of **total transactions**, **total procurement expenditure**, **total bottles procured**, **total cases procured** and **total volume procured liters**.
- **Sunday** has the **highest average price per liter** while **Wednesday** has the **least average price per liter**.
- **Wednesday** dominates in terms of **total procurement expenditure**, **total cases procured** and **total volume procured liters**.
- **Tuesday**, on the other hand, prevails in terms of **total transactions** and **total bottles procured**.  <br><br>
