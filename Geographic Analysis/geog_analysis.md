# Geographic Analysis

## Introduction

![County and City Count](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/county%20and%20city%20count.png)

A total of **100 counties** and **505 cities** are observed to be present in **Iowa state**. The geographical analysis attempts to showcase the very top counties and cities where procurement is at its absolute best in the period between 2016 and 2025. <ins>Core Question:</ins> *How does Iowa liquor procurement perform as viewed through the geographical lens?*

## Dominant Counties
The below figure shows the **top 10 counties** across the various procurement metrics:

![Top 10 Counties](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/county%20metrics.png)
[Tableau Link - County Analysis](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/County-KeyMetrics?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- The **top 3 counties (Polk, Linn and Scott)** consistently prevail in terms of **total transactions**, **total procurement expenditure**, **total bottles procured**, **total cases procured** and **total volume procured liters**. Particulary, **Polk** leads with **astronomical** figures.
- **El Paso** has the **highest average price per liter** which is slightly over **$20**.
- The **dominant counties** that appear across **all procurement metrics** are: **Polk**, **Johnson**, **Pottawattamie**, **Scott** and **Woodbury**.

## Prevailing Cities
The figure below indicates the **top cities** across the various procurement metrics:

![Top 10 Cities](https://github.com/AdenKabz/Iowa_Liquor_Procurement/blob/main/images/city%20metrics.png)
[Tableau Link - City Analysis](https://public.tableau.com/views/IowaLiquorProcurementAnalysis/CIty-KeyMetrics?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)
- The **top 3 cities (Des Moines, Cedar Rapids and Davenport)** consistently dominate in relation to **total transactions**, **total procurement expenditure**, **total bottles procured**, **total cases procured** and **total volume procured liters**. Additionally, **Des Moines** is the top most city with **very high** levels.
- In terms of **average price per liter**, the **top 10 cities** each have an **average price per liter** exceeding **$20**. Conversely, **Allerton** is the **most dominant city** with an **average price per liter** above **$60**. <br><br>
