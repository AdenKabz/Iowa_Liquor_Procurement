# Recommendations

## 📊✨ Data-Inspired Recommendations

A) **Operational and Supply Chain Optimization**
  * Dedicate most resources like staffing and logistical support toward **December** as it is the peak month dominating across all procurement metrics.
  * Significantly lower the procurement investment bet placed in **January** as it has lower figures in terms of bottles procured, volume liters and expenditure. Do the same for **February** as it has low transaction levels.
  * Capitalize on the **mid-week** for high-volume procurement. **Tuesday** should be used for frequency-driven metrics (transactions and bottle acquisition) while **Wednesday** is much suited for weight-driven metrics (expenditure, volume liters and case acquisition).
  * Due to very low procurement activity on **weekends (Saturday and Sunday)**, very minimal supply chain allocations should be dedicated to them.
  * Set aside a plan to cater for the high volume liters and bottles to be acquired as well as transactions to be undertaken during **Summer** while also organizing resources to handle the huge case acquisition and financial spending to be done during **Autumn**.
    
B) **Inventory Strategy**
  * Readjust supply chain budget to minimize resource wastage following the **post-2024** drop in all procumerent metrics.
  * Place much emphasis on restocking **12-packs and 6-packs** and most especially **12-packs** as they rank the highest across all procurement metrics.
  * Prioritize going for **750 ml and 1750 ml bottles** with much emphasis on **750ml bottles** as they are trend in the procurement world.
  * Make a deliberate effort to limit shelf space and capital investment toward the **ultra-premium tier (price per liter > $50)** as it ranks lowest in terms of transactions, bottle acquisition and expenditure.
  * Pour in more money into the **standard tier ($15-$30 per liter)** to get better value of investment while more shelf space should be dedicated to the **economy tier (below $15 per liter)** considering it attracts lots of transactions and bottle acquisition.

C) **Geography and Store Strategy**
  * Cleverly centralize the supply chain infrastructure to concentrate around the **top 3 counties (Polk, Linn and Scott)** and **top 3 cities (Des Moines, Cedar Rapids and Davenport)**. In particular, a heavy dosage of resources should primarily center around **Polk** and **Des Moines** as they rank highest across all procurement metrics.
  * Whether you are an investor looking to acquire or partner or the government looking to gain more revenue, targeting the **top 2 stores (Hy-Vee #3/ BDI/ Des Moines and Central City 2)** guarantees better results as they appear to have mastered the market due to their procurement prowess.

D) **Category and Item Strategy**
  * Give more preference to **American Vodkas** as it prevails across all procurement metrics.
  * Anchor inventory choice to the operational prowess portrayed by the **top 4 items (Black Velvet, Titos Handmade Vodka, Hawkeye Vodka and Fireball Cinnamon Whiskey)**. More capital expenditure should be devoted to **Titos Handmade Vodka** as it dominates in procurement expenditure. Acquiring more individual bottles should be dedicated to **Fireball Cinnamon Whiskey** as it ranks highest in bottle procurement. On the other hand, **Black Velvet** should used as the baseline for volume-driven procurement. <br><br>

## 📋 Concluding Remarks

The **Iowa procurement ecosystem** provides a revelation that the market has a high preference for **standard-economy price tiers**, **12-packs of 750ml bottles**, the **American vodkas category** and is heavily centered around the geographical hubs of **Polk county** and **city of Des Moines**. <br>
On a **month-to-month basis**, there is a cyclical tendency in the market. An operational expansion is witnessed from **January** to **June**, followed by a pivot toward high-value premium profile in the **Autumn season**; a great peak is then observed in **December**, but later a severe procurement drop occurs in **January** and **February**. **Weekly trends** indicate that heavy procurement concentration falls on **Tuesdays and Wednesdays** while the **weekends** are almost a fading memory. <br>
The **long-term growth** fuelled by increased spending and pricing across the years finally reached its peak in **2024** and then underwent a dip across all metrics in **2025**. With this in mind, **procurement managers** ought to revise their plan from **aggressive supply chain allocation** toward **cost-minimization** and **lean procurement management**.
