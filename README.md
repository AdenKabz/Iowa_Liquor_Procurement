# Iowa Liquor Procurement: A 10-Year Analysis

## 💬 Introduction ##
The **Iowa Liqour Procurement Analysis** covers a **10-year period (2016-2025)** showing the ordering patterns that drive store restocking in the liquor industry.

## 🔍📄 Background ##
The **Iowa state** through the **Iowa Alcoholic Beverages Division** calls the shots when it comes to **wholesale distribution** of distilled spirits. This strict enforcement ensures that no "Kill me quick" drinks (illicit liquor) finds its way to the end customer. The state buys liquor from the vendor and then sells it to private businesses (store retailers) who in turn get the products to their customers. This analysis zooms in and highlights one of the key aspects of the supply chain, that is, procurement. While revenue and profit display could act as justifiers for the procurement related numbers, that is not covered here. This is purely a procurement-centered discourse meant to uncover the purchase behavior of store retailers in general and understand the different procurement metrics involved.
